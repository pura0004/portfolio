ABOUT Jishnu Purahoo

Emily Hsueh is an Ottawa-based multimedia creator, currently enrolled at Algonquin College in the Interactive Media Design program. She has worked various jobs in the industry over the last few years, with a particular passion for photography and graphic design. Other industry skill she possesses include videography, web development, UX and UI design and professional writing.

IMD is Emily's second program; she is a graduate of the journalism program from both Algonquin College and the University of Ottawa. After receiving both her degree and diploma, Emily worked in Shawville - a small town in the rural quebec region of the Pontiac - at The Equity newspaper as a reporter, videographer and social media manager. But after a year in the newsroom, she decided she wanted to pursue a more creative career and returned to Algonquin.

When she is not working, you can find Emily doing what she does best: being creative. She has been an artist for as long as she can remember, mainly making illustrations digitally with Photoshop, as well as creating costumes and props for cosplay. She also loves to make music; she's been playing piano for 20 years and double bass for 8, and played with semi-professional orchestras in the past. By night, you can find her online in one of her favourite video games, chatting with friends and gaming the night away.
